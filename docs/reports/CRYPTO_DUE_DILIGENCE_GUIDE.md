### What This Means

Building a blockchain is one of the most complex software engineering tasks that exists. It requires:
- Consensus mechanism code

**You cannot build this in private and then magically release it.** Every legitimate blockchain has years of public development history.

**üîë Lesson:** If a "blockchain project" has no public code after months of fundraising, you're not investing in technology‚Äîyou're investing in a website.

## Part 2: The Wayback Machine Reveals Everything

The **Wayback Machine** (web.archive.org) is an investigator's best friend. It shows historical snapshots of any website.

### What I Found

I checked **19 snapshots** of the project's website over 2 years. Here's what I discovered:

#### The "Raised So Far" Counter

| Date | Claimed Amount | What Happened |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| Jan 2024 | $1.0M | Initial launch |

**Why would a legitimate project reset their fundraising counter to $0 multiple times?**

There's no innocent explanation. Either:
1. They're lying about how much they've raised
2. They're resetting to create artificial "fresh start" urgency
3. Their entire dashboard is manually controlled, not connected to real transactions

#### The Disappearing Partners

| Date | Partners Shown |
|------|----------------|
| Oct 2024 | Inter Milan, Borussia Dortmund, F1 teams |
| Jan 2025 | **BVB logo GONE** |

1. Go to `web.archive.org`

3. Click on dates 3-6 months apart
4. Screenshot everything
5. Compare: numbers, team, partners, promises

### Why Endless Presales Are a Red Flag

An endless presale means:
1. **No accountability** - Can't fail if you never "launch"
2. **Infinite fundraising** - Why stop when money keeps flowing?
3. **No real product** - A working product would be more profitable than presale
4. **Ponzi dynamics** - Later investors pay earlier investors' "returns"

**üîë Lesson:** If a presale has lasted more than a year, something is very wrong.

## Part 4: The Psychology They're Using Against You

Understanding these tactics won't just help you spot scams‚Äîit'll help you understand why smart people fall for them.

### Tactic 1: Artificial Scarcity

> "ONLY 3.55 BILLION COINS LEFT!"  
> "BATCH 26 SELLING OUT!"  
> "LAST CHANCE BEFORE PRICE INCREASE!"

**Reality check:** They control the supply. There's no actual scarcity.

### Tactic 2: Bonus Escalation

| Late 2024 | 100% (BULLRUN100) |

### Tactic 3: The Referral Pyramid

> "REFER FRIENDS & EARN 6-10% IN USDT!"

When a project pays you in stablecoins to bring in new investors, that's not marketing‚Äîit's a recruitment incentive. Your job becomes finding their next victim.

### Tactic 4: The Moving Target

Every time you ask "when mainnet?", the answer changes:
- "Q2 2024" ‚Üí "Q4 2024" ‚Üí "2025" ‚Üí "Soon" ‚Üí "Daily AMAs until TGE"

**If they could launch, they would have. They can't.**

## Part 5: The Community Is Weaponized

### How They Silence Critics

1. **Label them "FUD spreaders"** - Anyone with concerns is a "paid shill"
2. **Ban them quickly** - Dissent is removed within hours

Once you've:
- Told friends about your investment

- Were wrong

**This is why victims often become the loudest defenders.**

## Part 7: The 5-Minute Due Diligence Routine

Do this for EVERY crypto investment:

### Step 1: GitHub Check (1 minute)
```
‚Üí Search: github.com/[project-name]
‚Üí Look for: Recent commits, multiple contributors, open issues
‚Üí Red flag: No repo, or repo with only documentation/no code
```

### Step 2: Wayback Machine (2 minutes)
```
‚Üí Go to: web.archive.org/web/*/[project-domain]
‚Üí Check: 3 snapshots from different months
‚Üí Red flag: Changing numbers, disappearing partners, shifting dates
```

### Step 3: Audit Search (1 minute)
```
‚Üí Search: "[project] smart contract audit"
‚Üí Look for: PDF from CertiK, Hacken, Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin
‚Üí Red flag: No audit, or only "internal audit"
```

### Step 4: Liquidity Check (1 minute)
```
‚Üí Try: Add token to Uniswap/PancakeSwap
‚Üí Check: Is there actual liquidity?
‚Üí Red flag: Can't sell, or would lose 50%+ to slippage
```

### Step 5: The "Why" Question (30 seconds)
```
‚Üí Ask yourself: Why would this be underpriced?
‚Üí If thousands of people know about it, why isn't it already pumped?
‚Üí Red flag: "Early opportunity" that's been "early" for 2 years
```
